Title — Catch-Up or Keep-Up? The Heterogeneous Effects of Palermo Protocol Ratification on Anti-Trafficking Legislation

Abstract — This paper examines the effect of the staggered ratification of the Palermo Protocol on anti-trafficking legislation quality, measured by the 3P-Index covering prevention, protection, and prosecution policies. Using a country-year panel of 189 countries from 2000 to 2013 (Cho et al. 2014) and the pre-mean differenced Local Projections Difference-in-Differences (LP-DiD) estimator proposed by Dube et al. (2025), I find that ratification significantly improves overall anti-trafficking legislation quality, with a pooled average treatment effect of 0.775 index points. Effects grow over the five post-ratification years and are strongest for prosecution and protection, while prevention responds more modestly. Heterogeneity analysis reveals a catch-up dynamic: countries with weak rule of law prior to ratification drive the overall effect, while high rule-of-law countries show no significant response. These findings suggest that international un treaty ratification is most consequential where domestic institutional capacity is limited, consistent with a compliance mechanism whereby external legal commitments substitute for weak domestic enforcement incentives.

Keywords — difference-in-differences, LP-DiD, Palermo Protocol, human trafficking, 3P-Index, staggered adoption, event study, policy evaluation, heterogeinity analysis
